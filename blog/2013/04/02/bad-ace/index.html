This Is a Post. This Is Only a Post.

Apr 2nd, 2013

Wow, does this actually work?

So, it turns out that writing a blog post in markdown is actually kind of nice. I enjoy the simplicity of it. And the FoldingText app makes it even nicer. I feel I am about as close to a typewriter as I can get while working on a $3000+ shiny metal laptop.

Figuring out the templating process is a bit daunting, and I find myself wondering if I should be devoting time to yet another new thing, while my “real” website, fstopdesign.com languishes in its perpetually half finished state like the house of a contractor.

The designer in me badly wants to tweak and customize the design of this default template, but that’s where I stop short because I know I could spend days figuring out this system, and is it worth it?

I have finally gotten to a pretty fluent state with my WordPress theming and developing knowledge, so why would I want to go off and start over with something that’s totally new, and rather difficult to learn?

Well, the big attraction for me is the simplicity of the static site that these static site generators generate. I love WordPress, but honestly, the thought of serving up some simple, static, pre rendered HTML/CSS/JS files is very appealing after dealing with with the dynamic soup that is WordPress.

Then there is the fact that I’m actually, at this exact moment, doing something that I have been thinking about doing, urging myself to do, feeling bad that I have not been doing for a very long time: writing. It’s funny, I can feel my hands getting a bit tired from this little bit of typing. But it feels great!

Probably what I’ll do is let my inner designer suffer slowly as I continue to use this Octopress thing to write with first and figure out how to make it look like my own thing later.

I have to say, publishing a post with a rake deploy command in the terminal feels kinda cool.
